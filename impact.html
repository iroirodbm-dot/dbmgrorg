Our Impact

Transforming lives since 1987 — quality education, vocational training and community-led advocacy for the Deaf in Nagaland and beyond.

See Our Programs Support Our Work

At a glance

DBM runs the only residential School for the Deaf in Nagaland, provides vocational training and interpretation services, and has trained over 200 Deaf individuals to date. Below are quick facts and highlights from recent years.

Registered society, Dimapur, Nagaland

Impact highlights & success stories

DBM alumni and students have achieved recognition at state, national and international levels. We celebrate individual journeys that reflect the strength of inclusive education and skills training.

Mrs. Temsutola Jamir

DBM alumna and National Award recipient for Persons with Disabilities (2022). A leading example of achievement and advocacy for Deaf women in India.

Mr. Maongkaba Longkumer

First Deaf constable employed in the Nagaland Armed Police and represented India at the FIFA World Unified Cup (2013).

Ms. Samlam Chulio

1st Runner-Up, Miss Deaf India (2024) — one of many alumni who have excelled in arts and culture.

Mr. Aomeren

Recognised as the first Deaf matriculate in Nagaland — a milestone for access to secondary education for Deaf learners in the state.

Programs & outcomes

Our education and vocational programs produce measurable outcomes — education completion, employment, and independent livelihoods. Explore program details on the Programs page.

Education for the Deaf

Residential school providing free education (Nursery–Class X offline; Classes 11–12 online), teacher training and sign-language education in collaboration with SCERT Nagaland.

Learn more

Vocational Training

Weaving, tailoring, carpentry, food preservation, beauty care, computer literacy and many more trades; over 200 Deaf trained.

See programs

Interpretation & Outreach

Interpretation services for hospitals, police, courts and media, sign-language workshops and community awareness programs.

Certificate & Training Courses

Certificate Course on Sign Language (with SCERT) and FE-SEDE Foundation Course on Special Education in collaboration with SSA and RCI partners.

Annual activity snapshot

Selected highlights from our annual activity reports (2022–2024).

Annual Activity — 2022

  • Free education Nursery–Class 10 (offline); Classes 11–12 online.
  • Computer education for 31 Deaf; vocational training and teacher training.
  • Translated Book of Ruth into Naga Sign Language; free medical care to 70 students.

Annual Activity — 2023
